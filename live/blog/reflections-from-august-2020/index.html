Reflections From August 2020

Date coming soon

My favorite thing about August was the many one-on-one picnics with my friends there were. I will always choose 10 one-on-one picnics with my favorite people than 1 picnic with all of them.

On the 5th day of August, Bambi's spirit transitioned on to the next dimension. I was reminded once again of T.S. Eliot's Journey of the Magi: "This: were we led all that way for Birth or Death? There was a Birth, certainly, we had evidence and no doubt." Such comforting words if we meditate on them for long enough, but the pain of first hearing the news is forever imprinted in the memory of my consciousness.

My team and I concluded our Covid-19 project and are currently looking to have our work published. Working on this project reminded me of my insatiable curiosity and the pleasure I derive from attempting to solve problems that have potential for significant impact.

As I near the official end of my Stanford Seed Internship, I lament the ways the Covid-19 pandemic has disrupted our lives. No amount of technology can substitute the experience of working in a particular location. I love interacting with people at my place of work and learning through my continuous exposure to them.

This month I continued to reflect on the role of fear in my life. I have concluded that the overwhelming emotion in my life is not fear, it is grief. How have I adapted to the constant loss that has characterized my life for as long as I have lived? The key is contained in my favorite quote of all time: "O my soul, do not aspire to immortal life but exhaust the limits of the possible."

I intend to graduate in December instead of delaying to June 2021. Kicking the can to June is aspiring for immortal life. Life is made of transient moments. I am learning to allow beautiful moments to end when their time comes and to embrace new moments, with all of their uncertainty.
